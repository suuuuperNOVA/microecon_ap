The Production FunctionÂ¶

To produce goods or services for sale, a firm must transform inputs into output. The quantity of output a firm produces depends on the quantity of inputs. This relationship is known as the firmâ€™s production function.

Production function

Relationship between the quantity of inputs a firms uses and the quantity of output it produces.

Definitions

Before we start, there are some definitions to declare.

Total product/TP

Total output or quantity produced.

Marginal product/MP

Additional output generated by an additional input (worker).

M P = Δ Total Product Δ I n p u t s
Average product/AP

Output per unit of input.

A P = Total Product Units of Inputs
Marginal cost/MC

Additional cost incurred by an additional output.

M C = Δ C o s t Δ O u t p u t
Fixed cost/FC:

Cost that does not depend on the quantity of output produced. It is the cost of the fixed input.

e.g. rent, machines.

Variable cost/VC

Cost that depends on the quantity of output produced.

e.g. Cost of labor, which is equal to the number of workers multiplied by weekly pay.

Total cost/TC

TC = FC + VC

Average variable cost/AVC
A V C = V C Units of Outputs

Law of Diminishing Marginal ReturnsÂ¶

Important

The law of diminishing marginal returns states that:

As variable resources (workers) are added to fixed resources (ovens, machinery, tool, etc.), the additional output produced from each additional worker will eventually fall.
